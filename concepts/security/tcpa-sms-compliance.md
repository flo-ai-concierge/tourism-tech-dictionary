# TCPA SMS Compliance

## What it is
The Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA) is a US law that requires every commercial text message to include "Reply STOP to opt out." Violating it can cost $500-$1,500 per text in fines. It applies to any automated SMS sent for business purposes.

## Tourism Translation
**"Like the mandatory fire exit signs in every hotel room — legally required, non-negotiable, and the fine for skipping it is enormous."**

Every hotel room has fire exit signs and evacuation routes posted on the back of the door. No hotel thinks "our guests already know where the exits are, let's skip it." The signs are legally mandated, inspectors check for them, and the penalties for non-compliance are severe.

TCPA compliance is the same for SMS. Every automated text to a guest must include opt-out language. "Reply STOP to opt out" isn't optional, isn't a nice-to-have, and can't be skipped because "we only send helpful messages." The law doesn't care about intent — it cares about compliance.

## Why it matters

### Financial risk
At $500-$1,500 per text, sending 100 non-compliant messages to guests could result in $50,000-$150,000 in fines. Class action lawsuits are common in this space.

### Simple to implement
Adding "Reply STOP to opt out" to every outbound SMS template costs nothing and eliminates the risk entirely. There's no reason not to do it.
