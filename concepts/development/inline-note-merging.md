# Inline Note Merging

## What it is
Combining two separate data sources (like messages and internal notes) into one unified, chronological timeline for display. Both tables are queried independently, then their results are merged and sorted by timestamp so everything appears in order.

## Tourism Translation
**Like merging a guest's email thread and phone call logs into one unified communication history on the front desk screen.** Instead of checking two separate systems — one for emails, another for call notes — everything shows up in a single timeline sorted by when it happened. The front desk agent sees the full story without switching tabs.

## When you'll encounter it
- When different types of data (messages, notes, events) need to appear in one feed
- When building unified inboxes or activity timelines
- When users want a single view of all interactions regardless of source
