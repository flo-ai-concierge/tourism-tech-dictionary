# Gender-Neutral AI Output

## What it is
Instructing an AI to avoid all gendered pronouns (he/him/his, she/her/hers) in generated content. Instead, the AI uses the guest's first name or restructures sentences to avoid pronouns entirely. This avoids assumptions about a person's gender identity — important when the AI doesn't know the guest and the guest might be trans, non-binary, or particular about pronouns.

## Tourism Translation
**Like training front desk staff to say "Welcome, Alex! Your room is ready" instead of "Welcome, sir/ma'am."** The greeting is warm and personal without making any gender assumptions. In hospitality, getting someone's pronouns wrong creates an awkward moment. Avoiding pronouns altogether keeps every interaction comfortable and professional.

## Implementation
Instead of: "He was a wonderful guest who kept his property clean."
Write: "Alex was a wonderful guest who kept the property in great condition."

Key prompt instruction: "Use the guest's first name or restructure sentences. NEVER use he/him/his or she/her/hers. Avoid they/them for solo travelers (sounds impersonal)."

## Why it matters for STR
- Hosts leave public reviews visible to future hosts — wrong pronouns are embarrassing and permanent
- Airbnb/VRBO profiles don't always indicate gender
- Names can be gender-ambiguous across cultures
- Inclusive language reflects well on the brand
