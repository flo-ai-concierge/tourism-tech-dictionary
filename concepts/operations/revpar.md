# RevPAR (Revenue Per Available Rental)

## What it is
ADR multiplied by occupancy rate. It combines price and how often the unit is booked into a single performance number.

## Plain English
A rental charging $300/night but only 50% occupied has a RevPAR of $150. A rental charging $200/night at 80% occupied has a RevPAR of $160. The cheaper rental is actually outperforming the pricier one. RevPAR cuts through misleading ADR numbers.

## Tourism Analogy
A hotel with beautiful suites priced at $500/night but half-empty is less profitable than a modest inn at $200/night that's always full. RevPAR is the number that tells you which one is actually winning.

## Why it matters
Used in market underwriting and investment decisions. A high ADR with low occupancy is a red flag — RevPAR exposes that. A market with rising ADR but falling occupancy (like Beach Haven NJ in 2024–2025) shows supply outpacing demand.

## How it's used in FLOHOM
Part of the STR market data pulled via AirROI API into the Market Navigator's Underwriting criterion.
