# Fleet Score

## What it is
A percentage (0-100%) showing how complete the content or configuration is across all properties in a portfolio. Each property is scored individually on a checklist (has check-in steps? has media? has upsell products?), then all scores are averaged into a fleet-wide number.

## Tourism Translation
**"Like the hotel chain's quality audit — each property gets a checklist score, and the fleet average shows overall readiness."**

Marriott doesn't just inspect one hotel and call it done. Every property in the chain gets a quality score: Are the rooms clean? Is the lobby presentable? Is the pool maintained? Is the signage correct? Each property gets a percentage, and the regional manager sees the fleet average. Properties below 70% get flagged for attention.

A fleet score in a vacation rental platform works identically. Each property is checked: Does it have check-in instructions? Videos? Photos? Upsell products? Info release timing configured? The score tells the operator at a glance which properties are guest-ready and which need content work.

## Why it matters

### Prioritization at scale
With 15 properties (and growing), you can't remember which ones need work. The fleet score surfaces the gaps instantly.

### Accountability
A visible percentage next to each property name motivates completion. Nobody wants their property to be the lowest-scored one.

### Guest experience consistency
A high fleet score means every guest at every property gets the same quality experience — not just the ones at the property you happened to set up first.
