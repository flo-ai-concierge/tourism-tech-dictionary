# EF English Proficiency Index

## What it is
An annual global ranking published by Education First (EF) that scores countries and regions by how well their adult population speaks English. Scores are based on standardized English tests taken by millions of people worldwide each year. Countries are grouped into five tiers: Very High, High, Moderate, Low, and Very Low proficiency.

## Why it matters
It's the most widely cited independent benchmark for comparing English ability across countries — used by companies hiring international staff, governments designing education policy, and anyone asking "how well does Country X speak English?"

## 2025 Asia rankings (relevant to FLOHOM)

|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Singapore | ~610 | Very High |
| 2 | Philippines | 569 | High |
| 3 | Malaysia | ~560 | High |

The Philippines ranks **#2 in all of Asia** — above India, Japan, South Korea, and every Southeast Asian country except Singapore. English has been a co-official language of the Philippines since the 1987 Constitution and is the primary medium of instruction in schools from elementary level up.

## Tourism translation
Think of it like the hotel star-rating system, but for how well a country's workforce speaks English. A 5-star hotel guarantees a certain service standard — a "Very High" EF rating guarantees a certain communication standard. When you're hiring remote staff, this ranking is a fast, credible shorthand for "can they hold a professional English conversation?"

## Practical use
When Brian asked whether the Filipino GRVAs could handle English-language phone calls, the EF Index gave a sourced, verifiable answer: the Philippines is #2 in Asia for English proficiency — stronger than India, Japan, or any other Southeast Asian country except Singapore. More credible than just saying "yes they can."
