# Market-Aware Routing

## What it is
Sending messages from the phone number or email that matches the guest's property region. A Baltimore guest gets a text from the Maryland number, a Virginia Beach guest from the VA number. The system automatically picks the right sender based on property location.

## Tourism Translation
**"Like having a local concierge desk at each marina instead of one central number — guests see a familiar area code."**

Imagine calling a hotel and seeing a local area code vs a 1-800 number. The local number feels more personal, more legitimate, less like a mass marketing operation. Guests are more likely to answer a call or read a text from a number that looks like it's from their destination.

Market-aware routing makes this automatic. The system knows which property the guest booked, maps it to a market region, and selects the matching phone number — all without the operator thinking about it.

## Why it matters

### Higher response rates
Texts from local numbers have higher open and response rates than texts from unfamiliar area codes or short codes.

### Brand consistency
Each market has its own identity. Baltimore guests expect Maryland-area communications. Virginia Beach guests expect Virginia-area communications.

### Zero operator effort
The mapping is configured once (property city to phone number) and applies forever to every guest at every property.
