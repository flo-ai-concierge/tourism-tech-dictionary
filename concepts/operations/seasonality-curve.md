# Seasonality Curve

## What it is
A month-by-month chart or dataset showing when a market is busy (peak), moderate (shoulder), and dead (off-season). It maps demand across 12 months so you can see the rhythm of a market.

## Plain English
Every vacation market has a personality. Beach towns explode in summer and go quiet in winter. Ski resorts flip that. Mountain foliage markets peak in October. A seasonality curve is just that pattern visualized — it shows you exactly which months generate revenue and which months the property sits empty.

## Tourism Analogy
A ski resort's calendar: December–March is packed, April–May is dead, June–August is moderate hiking season, September is slow. A beach town like Beach Haven NJ is the opposite: July–August is 85%+ occupancy, January is 5%. Knowing the curve before you invest tells you whether the property can cover its costs year-round or only survives on 8 weeks of summer.

## What it tells you
- Which months to price high vs. discount
- Whether a market can support year-round operations
- How much of annual revenue is concentrated in the peak window
- Whether shoulder season is growing or shrinking (competitive signal)

## Beach Haven NJ example (May 2026 data)
- Peak: Jul–Aug, 84–88% occupancy, $370–$382 ADR
- Shoulder: May–Jun, Sep, 30–52% occupancy
- Dead: Oct–Apr, often under 10% in Jan–Feb
