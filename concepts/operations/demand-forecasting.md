# Demand Forecasting

## What it is
Using historical booking data, local events, seasonality patterns, and market signals to predict how many bookings a property will receive in a future period. Accurate forecasts let you raise prices when demand will be high and lower them (or run promotions) when demand will be low.

## Tourism Translation
**"Like a beachfront resort knowing July 4th weekend books out 3 months early, so they raise rates in April — predicting the wave before it hits."**

Your Myrtle Beach property always sells out for Memorial Day weekend. A good demand forecast knows this from historical data and starts raising the nightly rate 60 days out instead of waiting until the last week. It also knows that the week after Labor Day drops 40%, so it prices lower to attract the stragglers. The best forecasts also factor in one-off events — a music festival, a hurricane forecast, a new competitor listing — that history alone wouldn't predict.
