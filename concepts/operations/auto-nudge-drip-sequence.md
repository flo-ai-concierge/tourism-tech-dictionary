# Auto-Nudge / Drip Sequence

## What it is
Automated reminder messages sent on a schedule relative to an event. "T-7" means 7 days before, "T-1" means 1 day before. The system checks a clock, compares it to upcoming dates, and sends the right message at the right time — no human involvement once configured.

## Tourism Translation
**"The hotel sending 'looking forward to hosting you' a week out, then 'don't forget to check in online' the day before."**

Every good hotel has a pre-arrival communication sequence: confirmation email after booking, a "your trip is coming up" reminder a week before, a "here's how to check in" message the day before, and a "welcome, here's your room" on arrival day. Each message is triggered by the calendar, not by a person remembering to send it.

Auto-nudge systems automate this exact pattern. Define the schedule once (7 days = welcome, 2 days = reminder, 1 day = urgent), and the system handles every guest for every property forever.

## Why it matters

### Consistent guest experience
Every guest gets the same professional communication sequence regardless of which property, which season, or how busy the team is.

### Check-in completion rates
Guests who don't complete online check-in miss access codes, property details, and upsell offers. Timely reminders dramatically increase completion rates without staff effort.

### Respect limits
Good drip sequences have caps (e.g., max 3 reminders per guest) and skip guests who already completed the action. Nobody likes being nagged after they've done what was asked.
