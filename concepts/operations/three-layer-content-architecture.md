# Three-Layer Content Architecture

## What it is
Organizing guidebook content into three layers based on how it varies across properties:
- **Layer 1: Brand** — identical everywhere (About FLOHOM, Meet the Team, Music Playlist)
- **Layer 2: Property** — same title structure but content is unique per FLOHOM (WiFi, parking, rules)
- **Layer 3: Market** — shared by all properties in the same geographic area (Voyager Guides: restaurants, activities, local tips)

## Tourism translation
Like a hotel chain with:
- **Brand standards** (Layer 1): the welcome letter, loyalty program brochure, and brand story card are the same in every room worldwide
- **Room-specific info cards** (Layer 2): the WiFi password, thermostat instructions, and emergency exit map are different for each room
- **City guides** (Layer 3): the "Best Restaurants in Baltimore" booklet is the same for every room in Baltimore, but different from the Virginia Beach version

## When you'll encounter it
- Adding a new FLOHOM: Layer 1 copies word-for-word (13 entries), Layer 2 copies structure but needs property details (~13 entries), Layer 3 copies all Voyager Guides from the same market (6-14 entries)
- Editing guidebooks: understanding which layer you're in tells you whether the change affects one property, one market, or all properties

## The numbers
- Layer 1: 13 entries, all 15 properties, zero edits when copying
- Layer 2: ~13 entries, content unique per FLOHOM
- Layer 3: 6-14 entries per market, shared across all properties in that market
- Total for new property: ~30-40 entries, only ~13 need manual edits
